# DESIGN & UX/UI AUDIT — Ecohome.net

## Visual Design Assessment

### Overall Aesthetic
**Dated and generic.** The site looks like a 2015-era WordPress build. The carousel hero, stock photography, and dense text blocks feel like a template. While functional, it lacks the premium feel expected for North America's leading green building resource.

### Color Usage
- Primary coral red (#F05240) used effectively for CTAs and links
- Too much white space with no texture or depth
- Footer dark gray (#3F3F3F) is flat and uninspired
- No gradient, pattern, or visual texture anywhere
- Color palette is limited — needs warmer earth tones to match the "eco" positioning

### Typography
- **Raleway** for headings — overused Google Font, lacks personality
- **Open Sans** for body — on the BANNED font list, extremely generic
- No typographic hierarchy beyond size changes
- Body text at default gray (#707070) on white — adequate but uninspiring

### Spacing & Layout
- Standard 3-column grid, predictable throughout
- Dense body text block in the middle of the homepage is overwhelming
- No asymmetry, overlap, or visual surprise anywhere
- Every section follows the same pattern — monotonous

### Image Treatment
- Hero carousel shows AI-rendered homes — decent quality
- Card images are stock photography — generic construction workers
- No consistent image treatment (filter, overlay, aspect ratio)
- Images lack emotional impact

## User Experience Assessment

### Navigation
- ⚠️ Mega-menu with too many items — overwhelming
- ⚠️ "Find Professionals" / "Find Inspiration" / "Find Products" — three "Find" items
- ✅ Search bar is prominent
- ❌ Mobile navigation needs significant improvement

### CTA Visibility
- ⚠️ "Get started" button is small and unclear about what it does
- ⚠️ "Read more" buttons in carousel are the only hero CTAs
- ❌ No clear primary CTA on homepage (what should the visitor DO?)

### Trust Signals
- ⚠️ Mike Reynolds quote is buried below the fold
- ❌ No visible testimonials, partner logos, or social proof above fold
- ❌ No stats (articles published, homes helped, years of experience)

### Mobile Experience
- ⚠️ Navigation collapses but mega-menu is clunky on mobile
- ⚠️ Hero carousel is too large on mobile
- ⚠️ Text blocks are too dense for mobile reading

## Conversion Optimization Issues
1. No clear value proposition visible within 3 seconds
2. No primary CTA above the fold
3. No social proof near decision points
4. Dense text instead of scannable content
5. "Get started" button — started with what? Unclear.

## Key Opportunities
1. Single powerful hero with clear value prop and CTA
2. Trust bar with stats (articles, years, homes)
3. Visual guide categories with beautiful imagery
4. Testimonials near CTAs
5. Consistent, premium image treatment throughout
6. Interactive tools to drive engagement and return visits
